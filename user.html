User Research Projects

User Research is a way of generating insights about your user's behaviors, motivations, and needs. When it comes to designing, this kind of research is critical for success. Having user insight and testing helps the designer gain background knowledge on what the user wants to see. Throughout my studies at James Madison University, we have used User Research to create projects that fix a major design problem. Below are a couple of projects I created using User Research methods.

Bunkmates!



Bunkmates! User Research

This project was created in my SMAD 317: Interactive Design class. User Research allows for the designer to use suggestions from users instead of waiting for feedback after production. For my user research project, my group focused on the design problem; "College students need to find a better way to connect with other students when looking for roommates or friends with similar qualities as theirs". How we went about our research is through facetime interview calls and in-person interviews. We found 6 students from different majors and schools for our data. Once the research was conducted, we created the final guide on Indesign. This guide can be found linked above.


Each User Research project needs a prototype or example for viewers to understand how your product will work. For this assignment we created a prototype of our app, Bunkmates! We designed it using XD, and that can be found linked below or on the Adobe page.

Pocket Librarian



Pocket Librarian User Research

This project was created in my SMAD 203: Foundations of User Experience Design class. User Research is an important step in designing any app or website. It is the backward process of starting from the information from the user instead of waiting for feedback later. For my user research project, I focused on the design problem, "How might we improve the experience of JMU students trying to find cheap and affordable books. How I went about my research is through facetime interview calls. I found 7 students from different majors and backgrounds and began to ask the design questions; outlined in the project. Once I conducted my research I was able to create this user research finding guide with InDesign. The design outlines my design problem, design questions, approach, interview findings, and personas.


Each User Research project needs a prototype or example for viewers to understand how your product will work. For this assignment I created a prototype of our app, Pocket Librarian. I designed Photoshop, and that can be found linked below.
